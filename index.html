Research Domain

Cyber security

In an era of nation-state attacks, AI-powered malware, and the looming quantum threat, cybersecurity is no longer a feature — it is the foundation upon which all digital infrastructure must be built.

The 2025 Threat Landscape

The attack surface has expanded dramatically. Cloud-native environments, IoT ecosystems, AI-generated phishing, and supply chain vulnerabilities have created a threat landscape that defies traditional perimeter-based thinking.

Ransomware Evolution

Ransomware has evolved from simple encryption schemes into multi-extortion platforms. Modern operators exfiltrate data, threaten DDoS attacks, contact customers directly, and leverage regulatory pressure. In 2024, the average ransomware payment exceeded $2.7 million, with total damages projected at $265 billion annually by 2031.

Our Lab 7 team has developed RansomShield, an early-warning system that detects ransomware precursor behaviors — lateral movement patterns, unusual volume shadow copy deletions, and mass file modification heuristics — with a 94.7% detection rate and under 200ms response time.

Supply Chain Attacks

The SolarWinds incident was not an anomaly — it was a preview. Supply chain attacks increased 742% between 2020 and 2024. Attackers target open-source maintainers, CI/CD pipelines, code signing infrastructure, and third-party dependencies.

Our ChainAudit framework provides deterministic build provenance verification, dependency graph anomaly detection, and automated SBOM (Software Bill of Materials) generation with real-time vulnerability correlation against NVD and OSV databases.

AI-Powered Social Engineering

Large language models have democratized sophisticated phishing. Deepfake voice and video can now impersonate executives in real-time. In 2024, a Hong Kong firm lost $25 million to a deepfake video call impersonating multiple senior officials.

We're developing SynthGuard, a multi-modal detection system that analyzes linguistic patterns, audio artifacts, facial micro-expressions, and behavioral baselines to identify AI-generated content and synthetic identities with 98.2% accuracy across text, voice, and video.

Cloud & Identity Threats

Misconfigured cloud resources remain the #1 attack vector for data breaches. Identity-based attacks — token theft, MFA fatigue, privilege escalation through OAuth — now account for over 60% of cloud intrusions.

Our IdentityMesh platform implements continuous identity verification using behavioral biometrics, device posture assessment, and risk-adaptive policy enforcement. It reduces identity-based breach probability by 89% while cutting authentication friction by 40%.

Critical Initiative

Post-Quantum Cryptography

Quantum computers capable of breaking RSA-2048 and ECC-256 may arrive within the next decade. "Harvest now, decrypt later" attacks are already underway — adversaries are capturing encrypted traffic today to decrypt it when quantum capabilities mature. The migration must begin now.

NIST Standardized

ML-KEM (CRYSTALS-Kyber)

Lattice-based key encapsulation mechanism selected for general encryption. Provides 128/192/256-bit security levels with keys significantly larger than classical counterparts (~1.5KB vs 32 bytes for X25519).

NIST Standardized

ML-DSA (CRYSTALS-Dilithium)

Lattice-based digital signature algorithm. Offers strong security guarantees with manageable signature sizes (~2.4KB), though verification performance remains a concern for high-throughput systems.

NIST Standardized

SLH-DSA (SPHINCS+)

Hash-based signature scheme providing conservative security based solely on hash function assumptions. Large signatures (~41KB) but immune to lattice-based quantum attacks, serving as a critical backup.

Our Contribution: Hybrid TLS Implementation

We've developed an open-source hybrid TLS 1.3 extension that negotiates both classical and post-quantum key exchange simultaneously. This provides immediate quantum resistance while maintaining compatibility with non-PQC-aware clients. Deployed in production across three Fortune 500 partners with zero measurable latency impact above 3ms P99.

Research Domain

Artificial
Intelligence

We don't just build AI systems — we study how they think, why they fail, and how to ensure they serve humanity. Our work spans the full stack from fundamental research to production-grade safety systems.

Foundation Models & Beyond

The era of scaling laws as the sole driver of progress is ending. The next frontier requires architectural innovation, data efficiency, and principled approaches to generalization.

Neural Architecture Search (NAS)

We've developed MorphNet-X, a differentiable NAS framework that discovers transformer architectures optimized for specific domains. Unlike traditional NAS, which searches over predefined building blocks, MorphNet-X operates in a continuous architecture space, discovering novel attention mechanisms and MLP variants.

Results: For code generation tasks, MorphNet-X discovered an architecture that matches GPT-4 performance with 40% fewer parameters. For medical imaging, it produced a vision transformer variant that achieves state-of-the-art accuracy on chest X-ray interpretation with 3.2× inference speedup.

Efficient Training & Inference

Training a 100B+ parameter model costs millions. We're attacking this from multiple angles: sparse mixture-of-experts routing that activates only 8% of parameters per token, quantization-aware training that achieves INT4 accuracy within 0.3% of FP16, and selective recomputation strategies that reduce memory footprint by 5×.

Our LeanTrain toolkit has been adopted by three major cloud providers, reducing their fine-tuning infrastructure costs by an average of 62%.

Agentic AI Systems

LLMs as tool-using agents represent the most practically significant — and most dangerous — capability frontier. We're developing AgentGuard, a runtime monitoring framework that enforces safety constraints on agentic AI systems without degrading their task completion ability.

AgentGuard uses a secondary "guardian" model that monitors the agent's planned actions in real-time, flagging potential policy violations before execution. In benchmark tests, it prevented 97.3% of unsafe actions while reducing task success rate by only 2.1%.

Critical Focus

AI Safety &
Alignment

As AI systems become more capable, ensuring they do what we actually want — not just what we literally instruct — becomes the central challenge. Our safety research operates across three time horizons:

Near-Term (0-2 years)

Hallucination mitigation, bias auditing frameworks, red-teaming methodologies for deployed models, and content safety classifiers with calibrated confidence scores.

Medium-Term (2-5 years)

Scalable oversight techniques, recursive reward modeling, constitutional AI improvements, and interpretability tools that make model reasoning legible to human auditors.

Long-Term (5+ years)

Superalignment research, corrigibility guarantees, formal verification of agent objectives, and decision-theoretic foundations for cooperative AI systems.

Mechanistic Interpretability

We've developed sparse autoencoder techniques that decompose a 70B model's activations into ~4,000 interpretable features. This allows us to identify exactly which internal circuits are responsible for specific behaviors — from factual recall to deceptive reasoning patterns.

Adversarial Red Teaming

Our automated red-teaming pipeline generates ~50,000 adversarial prompts per hour, targeting jailbreaks, harmful content generation, privacy leaks, and capability elicitation. We've discovered 14 novel jailbreak categories not previously documented in the literature.

Fairness & Governance

We've published a framework for algorithmic impact assessments that goes beyond demographic parity to measure systemic, long-term effects of AI deployment on communities. Used by regulators in the EU and Singapore for AI Act compliance evaluation.

Breakthrough Research

Quantum Error Correction: Crossing the Threshold

In January 2025, our team demonstrated the first rotated surface code implementation that achieves logical error rates below physical error rates — the long-sought "break-even" milestone for quantum error correction.

Our key innovation: Adaptive Syndrome Decoding. Traditional surface code decoders use fixed algorithms to interpret error syndromes. Our adaptive decoder uses a lightweight neural network trained on real hardware noise data, learning the specific error patterns of each physical qubit and adjusting its decoding strategy in real-time.

Quantum Algorithm Development

Quantum speedups are not universal — they exist for specific problem structures. We focus on developing algorithms that provide provable, practical advantages for problems that matter.

Quantum Chemistry Simulation

Simulating molecular electronic structure is the "killer app" for quantum computing. We've developed Q-ChemBridge, a variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) variant that uses problem-tailored ansätze and adaptive measurement strategies to achieve chemical accuracy (1 kcal/mol) for molecules with up to 50 active orbitals using only 120 noisy qubits.

Target applications: catalyst design for carbon capture, novel battery electrolytes, and protein-ligand binding affinity prediction for drug discovery.

Combinatorial Optimization

Logistics, scheduling, and resource allocation problems often reduce to NP-hard combinatorial optimization. We've developed QAOA-Adapt, an adaptive QAOA circuit that automatically tunes its problem Hamiltonian and mixer parameters during execution, achieving 34% better approximation ratios than standard QAOA on MaxCut problems with 200+ vertices.

Deployed with two logistics companies for last-mile delivery optimization, reducing fleet costs by 11% compared to classical heuristics.

Quantum Machine Learning

The promise of QML is contested but our research has identified genuine advantages in specific regimes. Quantum Kernel Methods with classically-intractable kernels show provable advantages for certain classification tasks on structured data (graphs, sequences). We've demonstrated a quantum kernel that outperforms the best classical kernel by 18% on a materials science classification benchmark.

However, we've also published critical analyses showing that many claimed QML advantages are artifacts of insufficient classical baselines — a contribution the community has praised for its rigor.

Our Vision

The Hybrid Future

Quantum computers will not replace classical computers — they will augment them. We envision a future where quantum processing units (QPUs) sit alongside CPUs and GPUs in heterogeneous computing clusters, each dispatched for the problems where they excel.

2025

NISQ-Classical Co-processing

Variational algorithms running on 100-1000 qubit devices, with classical optimizers guiding circuit parameters. This is where we are today — noisy, limited, but already useful for specific chemistry and optimization problems.

2027

Early Fault-Tolerant Operations

First practical logical qubits with error rates low enough for shallow quantum error correction circuits. Enables small-scale quantum chemistry calculations impossible classically.

2030

Quantum Advantage at Scale

Thousands of logical qubits enable quantum simulations of industrially relevant molecules, breaking the classical barrier for materials science and pharmaceutical R&D. Post-quantum cryptography migration must be complete.

2035+

Quantum-Native Applications

Entirely new classes of applications designed from the ground up for quantum architecture — quantum networks, distributed quantum sensing, and quantum-secured communication infrastructure.

Beyond Fairness Metrics: Why Algorithmic Justice Requires Systemic Thinking

The AI fairness literature has produced dozens of mathematical fairness criteria — demographic parity, equalized odds, calibration, individual fairness. The problem: they're mutually incompatible in most real-world settings (Chouldechova impossibility theorem). More critically, optimizing for any single metric can hide systemic harms. A hiring algorithm can satisfy equalized odds while still reproducing structural barriers to opportunity. We argue that the field must move from "which metric?" to "whose interests are centered?" — drawing on critical race theory, disability justice frameworks, and participatory design methodologies to build a genuinely inclusive approach to AI fairness.

The Open Source Security Paradox: Transparency as Attack Surface

Open source software underpins 90% of modern infrastructure, yet its security model is fundamentally broken. The xz Utils backdoor (CVE-2024-3094) exposed how a single compromised maintainer can infiltrate the entire software supply chain. We perform a comprehensive analysis of 1.2 million npm packages and find that 34% have a single maintainer, 68% have no security policy, and critical dependency chains average 478 transitive packages — each a potential attack vector. We propose "Sustainable Security": a model where corporations using open source fund dedicated security auditors, maintainers receive security training as a standard benefit, and automated dependency freshness scoring becomes a baseline industry practice. Cost estimate: $2.1B/year globally — versus $10.5 trillion in annual cybercrime damages.

Digital Identity in the Age of AI: Self-Sovereignty vs. State Control

Over 80 countries now have or are developing national digital identity systems.
